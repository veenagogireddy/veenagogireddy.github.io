    Research: Your Face is Your Wallet: Is Biometric Payment the Future?

    Detailed research on biometric payment technology and their limitations and future scope.
    Abstract

    People’s perception over money which is the heart of commerce to exchange goods and services is continuously changing. From bartering for transactions to precious metal coins, from cash to credit cards – the medium of money is ever shifting. Modern economy has been progressing at a pace where people can conduct transactions virtually at any time. For non-virtual transactions mobile payments with wallet applications are gaining their popularity. The next big thing in coming commerce sector is payment with biometrics, mostly with automated face recognition without cards and phone applications. This report discusses current payment systems around the world and provides a comparative analysis of payment system used in the USA, being a technologically advanced country and China, also being a technologically rich and being the pioneer in cashless economy. The report presents information and evidence regarding ‘payment with face’ – the very recent development that has started on a prototype basis in China and in the USA. To elucidate the technology behind face-payment, current three-dimensional facial recognition technology is broadly discussed. There are certain technology and perception challenges in the field of biometrics for which possible solutions to overcome the challenges have also come across the discussion.
